Classic British dish Shepherd's Pie

Shepherd's Pie is a classic British comfort food, traditionally made with minced lamb, though beef can be used in variations known as "Cottage Pie." This hearty dish layers a savory meat filling with vegetables like carrots, peas, and onions, all cooked in a rich, flavorful gravy. The filling is then topped with a generous layer of creamy mashed potatoes, which are often browned under the grill for a crispy, golden finish. Originally a dish to make use of leftover meat, Shepherd's Pie has evolved into a beloved staple of British cuisine. The combination of tender, seasoned lamb and buttery mashed potatoes makes it a satisfying and warming meal, especially popular during colder months. It's typically served on its own or accompanied by seasonal vegetables and a side of gravy for extra indulgence.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp (30ml) vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion (about 7oz- 200g) , diced
  • 3 garlic cloves , minced
  • 2 large carrots (about 12 oz - 350g) , peeled and diced
  • 1 ½ pounds (680g) ground lamb or ground beef
  • 2 tbsp (30g) tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp (20g) flour
  • 1 tsp (5g) salt,1/4 tsp (1g) pepper
  • 1 cup (240ml) beef broth
  • 1 tbsp (15ml) Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp fresh rosemary , finely chopped,1 tsp fresh thyme , finely chopped
  • 1 cup (150g) frozen peas
  • 2 pounds (900g) potatoes

Instructions

Prepare the meat filling
  1. In a large frying pan, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the onion and saute for 5 minutes. Add garlic and saute for 1 minute more.
  2. Add the diced carrots and diced celery and cook for a few minutes until slightly softened. Add ground meat and cook for 10-12 minutes or until the meat is browned. Break up the meat while cooking.
  3. Add flour and stir to coat, cooking for 1 minute more. Add tomato paste, beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, rosemary, thyme, salt and pepper. Simmer for few more minutes, until liquid is reduced and vegetables are tender.
  4. Stir in peas and then remove from heat.
Prepare Mashed potatoes
  1. Bring potatoes to a boil in a large pot with water that has been generously salted. Cook until the potatoes are perfectly tender. Drain the potatoes and return them in the hot pot on low heat and shake them around for a couple of minutes to remove the excess moisture.
  2. Using a potato masher, mash the potatoes with butter. Add milk and mix to combine. Season with salt, pepper and garlic powder. Add cheddar cheese and mix to combine.
  3. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  4. Transfer the meat filling into a 2 quart (2 liters) baking dish. Flatten the mixture using the back of a spoon or spatula.
  5. Place mashed potatoes on top of the meat filling.
  6. Bake for 30 minutes until golden brown
  7. Let sit for 10-15 minutes before serving.
